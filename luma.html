LUMA INSTITUTE

Students of the LUMA Institute’s Human-Centered Design Thinking Practitioner Certification program were asked to complete a final project that utilized the design thinking tools learned throughout the course. I chose to explore the topic of grief by identifying problem spaces for grieving individuals and communities, and using design thinking to come up with creative solutions and tools for navigating grief.

PROBLEM SPACE


While navigating the problem space I used MURAL and other design thinking frameworks to identify pain points, opportunities, and potential solutions. I utilized a recipe compiled of the following practices:
RESEARCH

  • Rose, Bud, Thorn
  • Affinity Clustering
  • Customer Journey
  • What’s on Your Radar
  • Stakeholder Map
IDEATION

  • Problem Tree Analysis
  • Round Robin
PROTOTYPING

  • Concept Poster
  • Story Boarding
  • Wireframing
FEEDBACK

  • Interviewing
  • Think Aloud Testing
  • System Usability Scale

TAKEAWAYS

My first idea was to develop a tool to help communities and grievers connect and offer support. However, when analyzing the customer journey, I realized that this tool would work for a community but may not be usable for someone who is grieving and operating from a limited capacity. I then shifted my focus to a specific subset of grievers, those who are acting as Executors of Estate for a loved one—something my mother was actively doing for my grandmother.

This led to a very tangible and usable solution space. Heirloom would be a virtual grief concierge assisting individuals and families in the process of end-of-life planning and executor of estate duties. There is a very real need for design work in this problem space that can lead to tangible and useful results that may transform the grieving process by minimizing logistics.

INSIGHTS

This project also inspired me to use design to engage with my own fear of death, also known as Thanataphobia. I am now working on developing an online community and resource hub for those who live with heightened death anxiety.

This website will host somatic tools for processing anxiety, resources for learning more about cultures of death around the world and throughout time, a forum to connect with peers, and an option to seek help for further care. The design will be living and breathing with nature elements that create a safe corner of the internet to sit with big emotions and find ways to be present.
